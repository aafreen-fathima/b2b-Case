The Idle Equipment Sharing Problem

A product exploration focused on helping small businesses share expensive equipment without the operational headaches.

Problem

Small producers — bakers, candle makers, woodworkers, food businesses — often own expensive equipment that sits idle 50–60% of the time.

They may want to share equipment with another nearby business, but existing solutions fail because:

scheduling becomes messy,
maintenance costs are unclear,
liability is uncertain,
and there’s no lightweight system built for ongoing B2B co-use.
Hypothesis

The problem is not finding a sharing partner.

The real issue is the lack of:

shared scheduling,
cost-split agreements,
and damage/liability protocols.

The coordination overhead makes most businesses avoid sharing altogether.

Validation Plan

Interview 8–10 small business owners in urban creator ecosystems like:

Montreal
Portland
Austin

Research channels:

Farmers markets
Facebook small business groups
Etsy/cottage food communities

Key question:

“What would make you trust another business enough to share equipment?”

MVP Strategy

Before building software:

Use a Google Form
Create agreement PDFs manually
Coordinate scheduling manually

Goal:
Validate whether businesses are willing to pay before investing in a platform.

V1 Scope

A lightweight tool that provides:

Shared scheduling
Auto-generated cost-split agreements
Simple damage protocols
Out of Scope
Marketplace/discovery
Payments
Insurance
Reviews
Core Insight

The opportunity may not be a marketplace — it may simply be a trust and coordination layer for small businesses sharing expensive equipment.
